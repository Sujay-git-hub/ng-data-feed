📦 EIA Natural Gas Storage Cycle — The Core Seasonal Engine

SeasonMonthsStorage ActionMarket ImplicationTrade Bias
Injection Season Apr – Oct Gas injected into underground storage. Weekly EIA report shows +40 to +100 Bcf/week builds. More supply going into storage = bearish price pressure. Large builds vs 5yr avg = down move. SHORT BIAS · Sell rallies · Avoid longs except Sep bottom
Withdrawal Season Nov – Mar Gas withdrawn from storage. Weekly EIA shows −50 to −250 Bcf/week draws. Storage depletion = bullish. Larger draws vs 5yr avg = spike up. Cold snaps = volatile breakouts. LONG BIAS · Buy dips · Hold swing longs

📅 Weekly EIA Report Trading Framework (Every Thursday)

Report vs EstimateSeasonExpected ReactionTrading Action
Injection LARGER than 5yr avg Apr–Oct Bearish confirmation. Price drops. Short on close above open. Target: prior support.
Injection SMALLER than 5yr avg Apr–Oct Bullish surprise. Short covering rally. Exit shorts. Can go long on bounce.
Withdrawal LARGER than 5yr avg Nov–Mar Bullish confirmation. Price spikes. Long on pullback after initial spike. Add to longs.
Withdrawal SMALLER than 5yr avg Nov–Mar Bearish surprise. Longs exit. Exit longs. Wait for reload lower.
Storage at 5yr HIGH (Oct end) Oct transition Large storage = weak winter setup. Bears pile in. Reduce long size in Q4 if storage above 5yr avg.
Storage at 5yr LOW (Oct end) Oct transition Tight supply going into winter. Bulls excited. Aggressive long Q4 if storage below 5yr avg heading in.

📋 Natural Gas "Short Interest Equivalents" — All Key Reports Traders Monitor

Just like equities have Short Interest Reports and SHO forms, NG has its own set of positioning, inventory, and flow reports. These are your market intelligence toolkit — all publicly free.

Report Name Publisher Frequency Release (IST) What It Tells You + Trading Use
① EIA Weekly Natural Gas Storage Report (WNGSR) EIA (US Govt) Weekly (Thu) 8:00 PM IST Thu Working gas in underground storage (Bcf). The single most important weekly data point. Compare to 5-yr avg and consensus estimate. Direct MCX price mover. Link: ir.eia.gov/ngs
② CFTC Commitments of Traders (COT) Report CFTC (US Govt) Weekly (Fri) 2:00 AM IST Sat Breakdown of NYMEX NG futures positions: Managed Money (hedge funds/speculators), Producer/Merchant (commercials/hedgers), Swap Dealers. Net Managed Money long/short = the closest NG equivalent to equity short interest. Extreme net-short = contrarian long; extreme net-long = contrarian short. Link: cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders
③ Baker Hughes Weekly Rig Count Baker Hughes Weekly (Fri) 11:00 PM IST Fri Number of active natural gas drilling rigs in the US. Rising rig count = more future supply = bearish (lagged 6–12 months). Falling rig count = supply tightening = bullish (lagged). A leading indicator, not immediate. Link: rigcount.bakerhughes.com
④ EIA Natural Gas Weekly Update EIA Weekly (Wed) ~11:30 PM IST Wed Comprehensive weekly summary: spot prices at Henry Hub and key hubs, LNG export volumes, pipeline flows, weather outlook, storage comparison. Best single-page NG briefing. Link: eia.gov/naturalgas/weekly
⑤ EIA Short-Term Energy Outlook (STEO) EIA Monthly ~10:30 PM IST (2nd Tue of month) Monthly forecast for NG prices, production, consumption, LNG exports for 18 months ahead. When EIA raises price forecast = bullish; lowers = bearish. Contains Henry Hub price forecast which directly anchors MCX. Link: eia.gov/outlooks/steo
⑥ CME/NYMEX Open Interest & Volume CME Group Daily ~7:00 AM IST (prev day) Open interest by contract month. Rising OI + rising price = new longs = bullish trend. Rising OI + falling price = new shorts = bearish trend. Falling OI = position liquidation. Monitor front-month vs next month spread (contango/backwardation) as supply signal. Link: cmegroup.com
⑦ NOAA Weather Forecasts (HDD/CDD) NOAA (US Govt) Daily / Weekly Continuous Heating Degree Days (HDD) and Cooling Degree Days (CDD) forecasts. The primary demand driver. Each HDD above normal = more gas burned for heating. 8–14 day outlook drives short-term NG price. Colder than normal Nov–Mar = bullish. Hotter than normal Jul–Aug = mild bullish (AC demand). Link: cpc.ncep.noaa.gov
⑧ EIA Natural Gas Monthly Report (NGM) EIA Monthly (~45 day lag) ~10 PM IST (mid-month) Detailed monthly data: production by state, consumption by sector, imports/exports, pipeline flows. Confirms or challenges STEO forecasts. Used for longer-term (weeks–months) positioning. Link: eia.gov/naturalgas/monthly
⑨ LNG Export Terminal Reports EIA / FERC Weekly Various US LNG vessel loadings and export volumes. As US LNG capacity grows, higher exports = less domestic supply = structurally bullish for Henry Hub. Key terminals: Sabine Pass, Freeport, Corpus Christi, Cameron, Cove Point. Track via EIA weekly or Bloomberg shipping data.
⑩ FERC Gas Pipeline Reports FERC Monthly ~45 day lag Pipeline capacity utilization, interstate flow data, constraint reports. Useful for understanding regional price differentials (basis). Less directly relevant for MCX but explains Henry Hub vs spot basis divergence.

🔗 MCX NG ↔ NYMEX Henry Hub Linkage — How Reports Flow Through to MCX Price

Chain StepWhat HappensTime Lag
1. NYMEX NG (Henry Hub) Benchmark global NG price. All EIA/CFTC reports directly move NYMEX. This is the source signal. Immediate
2. Asian LNG Spot (JKM) Asian LNG spot price (Japan/Korea Marker). Tracks NYMEX with LNG export premium. India imports LNG → JKM affects domestic gas pricing expectations. Correlated, 1–3 days
3. India Domestic Gas Price APM (Administered Price Mechanism) gas + GAIL pipeline gas. Revised semi-annually by MoPNG. Sets floor for India NG economics. 6-month revision cycle
4. MCX NG Futures Tracks NYMEX with ~85–92% correlation. MCX = NYMEX price × USD/INR rate + basis. Basis typically ₹10–30/MMBtu. During high-volatility NYMEX moves, MCX follows within hours. Same session or next day
Practical rule for MCX traders: Watch NYMEX NG front-month price at 6:00 PM IST (US pre-market) and again at 8:00 PM IST (EIA report time). If NYMEX moves >1.5% post-EIA, MCX will confirm it within the same evening session (closes 11:30 PM IST). The correlation is tight enough that NYMEX direction = MCX direction on 90%+ of trading days.

📌 COT Report Deep Dive — The "Short Interest" Equivalent for NG

Trader CategoryWho They AreHow to Use
Managed Money (MM) Hedge funds, CTAs, systematic traders. Pure speculators. Net position = market sentiment proxy. MM net-short at historic extreme = contrarian LONG signal (forced covering upcoming). MM net-long at extreme = contrarian SHORT signal. Most useful at turning points.
Producer/Merchant/Processor/User (PMPU) Gas producers, utilities, industrials. Hedge real positions. "Smart money" — they know supply/demand fundamentals. When PMPU are heavily long (unusual for them — normally short hedgers), it signals producers expect prices to rise. Bullish confirmation. Heavy PMPU short = they're locking in high prices = potential top.
Swap Dealers Banks, financial intermediaries. Counterparties to OTC swaps. Usually take the opposite side of PMPU. Less directional signal, more liquidity/flow indicator. Large swap dealer short = commercials are buying via OTC = bullish flow.
Key COT signals for MCX NG: Released every Saturday ~1 AM IST (Fri 3:30PM ET, reflects Tuesday positions). Use it as a weekly sentiment check. When Managed Money is at a multi-year net-short extreme AND the seasonal is bullish (Oct–Jan) = highest probability long setup. When MM is at net-long extreme AND seasonal is bearish (Apr–Aug) = highest probability short. It's the positioning confirmation layer on top of seasonal bias.
